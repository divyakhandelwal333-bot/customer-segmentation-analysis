* Customer Segmentation Analysis
 
* Project Overview
This project performs customer segmentation using data analysis and machine learning to identify distinct groups of customers based on their income and spending behavior.
The objective is to help businesses understand customer purchasing patterns, enabling more effective targeted marketing strategies, improved customer retention, and increased revenue.
Customer segmentation is widely used in retail and e-commerce industries to personalize marketing campaigns and maximize business growth.

* Business Problem
Businesses often apply the same marketing strategy to all customers, which leads to:
Inefficient marketing spending
Poor customer targeting
Lower conversion rates
Missed high-value custumer opportunities
The goal of this project is to identify meaningful customer groups so companies can implement data-driven marketing strategies.

* Dataset
The dataset contains customer information including:
Column	Description
CustomerID	Unique identifier for each customer
Age	Age of the customer
Annual Income	Customer's yearly income
Spending Score	Score assigned based on spending behavior

* Customer Segmentation (K-Means Clustering)
To identify distinct customer groups, K-Means clustering was applied using the following variables:
i Annual Income
ii Spending Score
The algorithm grouped customers into clusters representing different behavioral segments.

* Key Insights
The analysis revealed several important customer patterns:
• Customers with high income and high spending represent the most valuable segment.
• Some customers with high income have low spending, indicating untapped potential for targeted marketing.
• A group of low income but high spending customers shows strong engagement despite lower purchasing power.
• Most customers fall into a moderate income and moderate spending category.
These insights help businesses prioritize marketing efforts.
